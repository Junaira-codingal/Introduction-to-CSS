Unknown History

The Mystery of the Green Children of Woolpit

The Green Children of Woolpit is a mystery from 12th-century England. Two children with green skin suddenly appeared in the village of Woolpit. They spoke an unknown language and refused to eat regular food. Over time, their skin lost its green color, and they learned English. They claimed to be from an underground world called "St. Martin's Land," always in twilight. The mystery of their origin remains unsolved

The Mystery of the Bermuda Triangle

The Bermuda Triangle, located in the Atlantic Ocean, is infamous for mysterious disappearances of ships and airplanes. Over the years, several vessels and planes have vanished under strange circumstances in this region, with no clear explanation. Despite numerous theories—from natural phenomena to paranormal activity—no one has been able to definitively solve the mystery of why so many disappearances occur in this particular area.

The Door to Hell

The Darvaza Gas Crater, also known as the "Door to Hell," is a burning pit in Turkmenistan that has been alight since 1971. Scientists originally set fire to it to prevent the spread of methane gas, but the fire has continued to burn for decades. Despite several attempts to extinguish it, the crater remains a mysterious, fiery spectacle. The exact cause of the ongoing flames and the crater's true origins continue to baffle experts and attract visitors from around the world.

The Mystery of Stonehenge

Stonehenge, located in England, is one of the world's most famous ancient monuments. This prehistoric stone circle has puzzled scientists and archaeologists for centuries. The exact purpose of Stonehenge remains unknown, with theories ranging from a religious site to an astronomical observatory. How and why these massive stones were transported and arranged in such a precise manner still remains a mystery, leaving it one of the greatest unsolved puzzles of human history.

The Disappearance of D.B. Cooper

In 1971, a man known as D.B. Cooper hijacked a plane, extorted $200,000 in ransom, and then parachuted out of the aircraft, disappearing without a trace. Despite an extensive manhunt and years of investigation, Cooper’s true identity and his fate remain a mystery. The case remains one of the most baffling unsolved crimes in U.S. history, and his whereabouts or what happened to him after he jumped from the plane continues to intrigue people around the world.
